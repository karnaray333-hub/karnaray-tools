Why Should You Compress Your Images?

Image compression is the process of reducing the file size of an image while maintaining acceptable visual quality. There are many important reasons why you should compress your images before using them online or sharing them.

Website loading speed is one of the most critical factors for user experience and Google search rankings. Studies show that a website that loads in 1 second has a much higher visitor retention rate than a site that takes 5 seconds to load. Large uncompressed images are the most common cause of slow website loading times. By compressing your images, you can dramatically improve your website speed and Google ranking.

Benefits of Compressing Images

  • Faster website loading — Compressed images reduce page load time significantly, improving user experience
  • Better Google SEO ranking — Google uses page speed as a ranking factor; faster pages rank higher
  • Save storage space — Reduce storage usage on your phone, computer or cloud storage service
  • Easier file sharing — Smaller images are faster to send via WhatsApp, email and social media
  • Lower data usage — Compressed images consume less mobile data when uploading or downloading
  • Meet upload size limits — Many platforms have maximum file size limits for image uploads

Frequently Asked Questions

Will compressing an image reduce its visual quality?
At the recommended 75% quality setting, the visual difference between the original and compressed image is barely noticeable to the human eye. Only at very low quality settings below 50% does image quality become visibly degraded.
What image formats does the compressor support?
Our Image Compressor supports JPG, JPEG, PNG and WebP image formats. These are the three most widely used image formats on the internet today.
